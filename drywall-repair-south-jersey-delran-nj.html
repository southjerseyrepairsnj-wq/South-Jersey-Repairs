Delran Drywall Repair FAQ

My basement drywall is soft near the bottom of the wall. What causes that?

Soft or crumbling drywall near the base of a finished basement wall is almost always moisture wicking up from the concrete slab or through the block wall. The drywall needs to come out and the moisture source needs to be addressed - whether that is improving drainage outside or applying a waterproof membrane - before new drywall goes back in. We can handle the drywall portion and advise on what to address before we patch.

How many coats of compound do you apply?

At minimum three coats - a fill coat, a tape coat if needed, a finish coat - with sanding between each. Skipping coats is why so many DIY patches shrink, crack, and show through paint. We do not skip steps.
