Rental Turnover Checklist

Between tenants, focus on safety, cleanliness, function, and the small details that make the property feel cared for.

Before Cleaning

  • Remove left-behind furniture, trash, boxes, and debris.
  • Photograph damage, wall holes, broken blinds, stains, and missing items.
  • Check doors, locks, windows, smoke detectors, lights, fans, and fixtures.

Repair List

  • Patch drywall holes, touch up paint, repair trim, and tighten hardware.
  • Fix loose towel bars, curtain rods, closet shelves, cabinet doors, and handles.
  • Replace damaged caulk around tubs, sinks, toilets, and backsplashes.

Ready-to-Show Details

  • Pressure wash visible exterior areas when needed.
  • Handle junk removal and small curb appeal repairs.
  • Make sure rooms are bright, clean, functional, and easy to photograph.

Tenant Turnover Priorities

Start with safety and water issues, then move to function, then appearance. A clean, working property usually rents faster than one with obvious unfinished repair items.

Helpful Text Message

Send your town, move-in deadline, photos of each room, and a short list of known repairs. That makes it easier to estimate and schedule the work.
