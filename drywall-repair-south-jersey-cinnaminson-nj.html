Drywall Repair for Cinnaminson Homes

Ranch-style homes in Cinnaminson typically have low ceilings with original drywall or converted plaster from the 1950s and 1960s builds. Seams in these ceilings tend to crack and open as the home settles, especially in the living room and hallway where foot traffic and HVAC vibration are constant. We re-tape failed seams, skim the area flat, and sand before priming so the repair blends under paint rather than telegraphing a line.

Colonials from the 1970s and 1980s often show corner bead damage along outside corners - dents, separating metal bead, and cracked compound at the corner edge. We repair or replace corner bead and re-finish the corner properly.

Cinnaminson Drywall Repair FAQ

Can you fix a ceiling seam that keeps cracking in the same place?

Yes. A recurring ceiling crack in the same location means the underlying tape has let go. We cut it back, apply new mesh or paper tape, re-coat with compound in proper layers, and sand smooth. Patching over a failed seam without re-taping will crack again - we do it correctly the first time.

Do you repair corner bead damage?

Yes. Dented or separating metal corner bead on outside corners is a common repair in Cinnaminson homes. We assess whether the bead can be re-secured or needs to be replaced, then re-finish the corner with compound and sand clean.
