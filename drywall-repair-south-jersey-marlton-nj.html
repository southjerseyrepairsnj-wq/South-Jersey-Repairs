Marlton Drywall Repair FAQ

My basement walls are bubbling near the floor. Is that a drywall problem or a moisture problem?

Usually both. Drywall that is bubbling or soft near the floor of a finished Marlton basement is absorbing moisture from the concrete slab or block wall behind it. The drywall needs to be replaced, but if the moisture source is not addressed first, the new drywall will develop the same issue. We look at the condition of the wall before recommending a repair approach.

Do you fix garage drywall?

Yes. Garage drywall - particularly the wall between the garage and living space - is common repair work. We patch impact damage, holes, and deteriorated sections and can match the fire-rated drywall spec required for that wall.

How do you fix nail pops so they don't come back?

We don't just fill over nail pops. We drive a new screw near the pop to secure the drywall properly, then dimple the nail head below the surface, fill both, sand smooth, and prime. Done correctly, nail pops don't come back in the same spot.
