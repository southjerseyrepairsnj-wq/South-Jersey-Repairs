Seller Prep Repair Checklist

Before listing a home, focus on visible repairs, safety items, first-impression issues, and anything buyers may notice during a showing or inspection.

First Impression Repairs

  • Exterior door issues, loose trim, sticking doors, and damaged thresholds.
  • Fence repair, pressure washing, curb appeal cleanup, and junk removal.
  • Paint touch-ups, drywall patches, and visible wall damage.

Interior Punch List

  • Drywall holes, nail pops, caulk gaps, loose shelves, and cabinet issues.
  • Bathroom refresh items, minor plumbing repairs, fixture updates, and trim repairs.
  • TV mount holes, move-out marks, door hardware, and small flooring transitions.

Before Photos & Showings

  • Clear clutter and haul unwanted items.
  • Handle obvious unfinished repairs before listing photos.
  • Bundle small repairs so the home feels maintained and move-in ready.

Best Seller Prep Priorities

Start with repairs that show in photos, items that suggest neglect, and issues around doors, walls, bathrooms, floors, exterior cleanup, and storage areas.
